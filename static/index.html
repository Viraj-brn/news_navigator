India's Union Budget 2025: A Complete Synthesis for Investors & Markets
📰 Synthesized from 14 ET articles 🕐 Generated 8 min ago ⏱ 7 min read
⚡ The Core Announcement — What Changed ▾
The Finance Minister revised the STCG tax on equity to 20% (from 15%) and kept LTCG unchanged at 12.5% with ₹1.25L exemption. Crucially, STT on futures & options was hiked by 0.02%, directly impacting retail derivative traders. The new tax regime saw standard deduction raised to ₹75,000 — benefiting salaried individuals choosing NTR.
📊 Mutual Fund Implications — Your Portfolio ▾
Debt mutual funds will now be taxed at slab rates regardless of holding period — a significant change from the previous indexation benefit. Equity-oriented hybrid funds retain favorable LTCG treatment. Gold ETFs and International Fund of Funds face new grandfathering rules with a 24-month holding threshold for LTCG.
🏦 Market Structure & FII Impact ▾
Initial market reaction was a 1,100-point Sensex drop, recovering 60% by close. FII desk sentiment on ET sources cited the F&O STT hike as likely to reduce speculative volumes by 15-20%. However, the ₹11.1L crore capex outlay was seen as a positive long-term signal for infrastructure-linked equities.
🔍 Contrarian Perspectives ▾
Several ET opinion pieces argue the STCG hike signals a structural shift towards "long-termism" in Indian investing — potentially healthy for the market's maturity. Counter-argument from derivatives desks: reduced F&O volumes could thin liquidity in index options, ironically increasing volatility rather than reducing it.
🎯 What To Watch Next ▾
Watch for: (1) AMFI data release on Aug 1 showing whether SIP flows hold post-budget. (2) RBI MPC meeting — budget fiscal math leaves headroom for rate cut. (3) F&O volumes in the next 2 weeks as a leading indicator of retail participation shift.

🇬🇧 Original — English
The Finance Minister on Tuesday announced a hike in Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) tax on equity from 15% to 20%, while Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax remains unchanged at 12.5% with an exemption limit of ₹1.25 lakh.

Analysts at ET suggest this move signals a regulatory push toward longer holding periods and may moderate the speculative froth seen in derivative markets over the past 18 months.
